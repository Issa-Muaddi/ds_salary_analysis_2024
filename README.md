Project Overview 
This project analyzes Data Science salaries in 2024 across various factors such as job title, company size, experience level, country, and remote work ratio.
It uses visualizations like bar plots, boxplots, pie charts, line charts, heatmaps, and word clouds to explore the dataset and highlight trends.

Data Source:
 Dataset: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/yusufdelikkaya/datascience-salaries-2024?utm_source=chatgpt.com

The dataset contains columns like: work_year, experience_level, employment_type, job_title, salary, salary_currency, employee_residence, remote_ratio, company_location, company_size.




Insights:
Full-time employees (FT) represent the majority of the workforce.
USA and Switzerland have the highest average salaries.
Larger companies tend to pay higher average salaries.
Salary trends show a gradual increase from 2020 to 2024.


Visualizations:

1)Top 10 Jobs by Average Salary (Bar Plot)
Highlights the highest paying roles.
Insight: ML Engineer and Data Scientist are among the top-paying jobs.
2)Top 10 Countries by Average Salary (Bar Plot)
Shows countries with the highest average salaries.
Insight: USA and Switzerland have the highest salaries.
3)Average Salary by Experience Level (Bar Plot)
Compares salaries across Junior, Mid, Senior levels.
Insight: Higher experience leads to higher average salaries.
4)Salary Distribution (Histogram)
Shows overall salary distribution (in thousands USD).
Insight: Most salaries are in the low-to-mid range; few high salaries skew the distribution.
5)Employees by Employment Type (Pie Chart)
Distribution of Full-time, Part-time, Contract, etc.
Insight: Most employees work full-time; small categories grouped under "Other".
6)Employees by Company Size (Pie Chart)
Shows number of employees by Small, Medium, Large companies.
Insight: Medium and Large companies employ most people.
7)Salary Distribution by Remote Work Percentage (Boxplot)
Compares salaries based on remote work (0%, 50%, 100%).
Insight: Salaries vary with remote work, but most are around the median.
8)Average Data Science Salary Trend (2020-2024) (Line Chart)
Tracks salary changes over the years.
Insight: Average salaries increased steadily from 2020 to 2024.
9)Most Common Job Titles in Data Science (Word Cloud)
Displays most frequent words in job titles.
Insight: Data Scientist, ML Engineer, and Data Analyst are most common roles.
10)Correlation Between Numerical Features (Heatmap)
Shows correlation among salary, salary_in_usd, and remote_ratio.
Insight: Salary in USD strongly correlates with salary; remote_ratio has a weak correlation.
